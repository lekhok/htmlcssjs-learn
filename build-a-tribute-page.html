Srimanta Sankardev | (শ্ৰীমন্ত শংকৰদেৱ)

Sankardev, also known as Mahapurusha Sankaradeva, was a 15th-century saint, poet, and philosopher who significantly contributed to the Bhakti movement in Assam, India. He was a multi-talented personality who excelled in various fields like literature, music, dance, and drama. Sankardev founded the Ekasarana Dharma, a religion that emphasizes the devotion of a single God, and promoted the use of the Assamese language in religious practices, which eventually led to its development as a literary language. He also played a crucial role in the social reforms of his time, advocating for the upliftment of women, the abolition of untouchability, and the promotion of human dignity. Sankardev's legacy continues to inspire people in Assam and beyond, and his contributions to literature, culture, and society remain unparalleled.
